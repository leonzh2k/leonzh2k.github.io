The Game of Life (Unfair Game)

There are two players. The player whose turn it is rolls the dice. The total of the roll is added to the current player's score, and if you roll non-one doubles,
you can roll again or pass. Rolling again after getting doubles (a doubles re-roll) has a higher chance of getting more points, but also a higher risk of rolling
snake eyes. A doubles re-roll, from either player, increases the risk/reward factor. This factor is reset once a player rolls snake eyes from a doubles re-roll.

Because life is unfair, if you roll two "ones" (snake eyes), you lose half of your points.
